BERKSHIRE HATHAWAY INC.

To the Shareholders of Berkshire Hathaway Inc.:

This is my first letter to you as CEO of Berkshire Hathaway. Warren Buffett remains our Chairman and is in the office five days a week, as involved and energetic as ever. His wisdom continues to guide our decisions. But as he indicated last year, he will no longer be writing these letters or speaking at length at our annual meeting.

I will do my best to continue the tradition of honest, direct communication that Warren and Charlie Munger established over six decades.

2025 Results

Berkshire produced $46 billion of net cash flows from operating activities in 2025, comparing favorably with our five-year average of more than $40 billion. Our property-casualty businesses achieved a combined ratio of 87.1%, better than our five-year average of 90.7%.

The year presented challenges. Significant wildfire-related losses in Los Angeles affected our results in the first quarter. However, the Atlantic hurricane season was unusually benign—for the first time in a decade, no hurricane made landfall in the United States. Our underwriters maintained discipline throughout, and our overall insurance results were strong.

Acquisitions and Capital Deployment

The Lubrizol team, led by Rebecca Liebert, was integral to our acquisition of OxyChem and its planned integration as a standalone operating business within Berkshire. This acquisition exemplifies what we seek: a well-managed business with durable competitive advantages, available at a fair price.

We continue to hold substantial cash reserves. Warren has always emphasized that we will deploy capital when opportunities arise, but we will not stretch for activity's sake. Patience remains our competitive advantage.

Leadership Changes

Marc Hamburg, who has served Berkshire with distinction for decades, will retire on June 1, 2026. Chuck Chang will succeed him. We also welcomed Mike O'Sullivan as Berkshire's first General Counsel.

These transitions reflect our commitment to continuity and long-term thinking. Berkshire's culture does not depend on any single individual. Our values—integrity, decentralization, long-term orientation—are embedded in everything we do.

Insurance Operations

Property-casualty insurance remains the foundation of Berkshire. Our float now exceeds $170 billion, and we generated underwriting profits once again. GEICO continues its turnaround under Todd Combs's leadership. Ajit Jain's reinsurance operations delivered outstanding results despite the challenging catastrophe environment early in the year.

Our underwriting philosophy is unchanged: we will write business at adequate prices or we will write no business at all. Discipline is not negotiable.

Looking Forward

Warren built Berkshire over sixty years by following simple principles: invest in wonderful businesses, maintain fortress-like financial strength, and treat shareholders as partners. My job is to preserve and extend what he built.

I am fortunate to have Warren as Chairman, available every day as we underwrite insurance, operate our businesses, and deploy capital. His guidance is invaluable.

Berkshire is in excellent condition. Our businesses are strong. Our balance sheet is unmatched. Our culture is intact. We are built to endure.

I look forward to meeting many of you at our annual meeting in Omaha.


Greg Abel
Chief Executive Officer

February 28, 2026
