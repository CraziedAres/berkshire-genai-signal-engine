BERKSHIRE HATHAWAY INC.

To the Shareholders of Berkshire Hathaway Inc.:

Charlie and I are pleased to report that 2021 was a good year for Berkshire. Operating earnings reached a record $27.5 billion, surpassing our previous high.

Our four giants—our insurance operations, BNSF, Berkshire Hathaway Energy, and Apple—all performed admirably. Together, they account for the vast majority of Berkshire's value.

Operating Businesses

BNSF hauled record tonnage as the economy recovered from the pandemic disruptions. Supply chain challenges actually benefited rail versus trucking in some corridors. Operating margins improved despite inflationary cost pressures.

Berkshire Hathaway Energy continued its steady growth, investing heavily in renewable generation and transmission. The energy transition creates both challenges and opportunities for utilities. We believe BHE is well-positioned for the decades ahead.

Our manufacturing, service, and retailing operations bounced back strongly. Many of our businesses set records for revenue and earnings. The resilience of the American consumer continues to amaze.

Stock Repurchases

We repurchased $27.1 billion of Berkshire stock in 2021, another record. We will continue to repurchase shares when we believe they are selling below Berkshire's intrinsic value and when we have no better use for our cash.

The math of repurchases is simple: if you buy dollar bills for 90 cents, continuing shareholders benefit. The key word is "intrinsic"—we are not interested in supporting the stock price, only in buying when the discount to value is clear.

Market Observations

The stock market in 2021 was buoyant. Low interest rates and massive fiscal stimulus drove equity valuations higher. SPACs proliferated. Meme stocks captured headlines. Cryptocurrencies soared.

Through it all, we stuck to our knitting. We found few attractive opportunities among publicly traded stocks. The prices demanded by sellers of private businesses remained elevated.

This is not a complaint. It is simply a statement of fact. When capital is abundant and fear is scarce, bargains are rare.

Cash Position

We ended the year with $146.7 billion in cash and Treasury bills. We would much prefer to put this money to work in productive assets. But we will not stretch for yield or compromise on quality.

Patience is a virtue in investing. We have demonstrated this repeatedly over sixty years. We see no reason to change now.


Warren E. Buffett
Chairman of the Board

February 26, 2022
