BERKSHIRE HATHAWAY INC.

To the Shareholders of Berkshire Hathaway Inc.:

Berkshire had a good year in 2022. We were particularly active in deploying capital, something that has been difficult in recent years.

We acquired Alleghany Corporation, a property-casualty reinsurer led by Joe Brandon, for $11.6 billion. Alleghany is exactly the kind of business we seek: well-managed, with a long track record, and available at a fair price. Joe and his team will operate independently within Berkshire, just as all our subsidiary managers do.

We also increased our stake in Occidental Petroleum significantly. We now own 21% of the company and have warrants to purchase additional shares. Oil and gas will be needed for decades, and Occidental's management has been outstanding in improving the company's cost structure.

Capital Deployment

In total, we deployed over $51 billion in 2022—our most active year ever for capital deployment. This included:

- The Alleghany acquisition
- Occidental Petroleum stock purchases
- Net purchases of other equities
- Berkshire share repurchases of $7.9 billion

For the first time in years, the phone was ringing with opportunities. Rising interest rates and falling asset prices created the kind of environment we have patiently awaited.

Operating Results

Operating earnings were $30.8 billion, up from $27.5 billion in 2021. All major segments contributed to the improvement.

Insurance results were particularly strong. Float reached $164.1 billion, with an underwriting profit of $2.6 billion. GEICO returned to growth after a period of remediation.

BNSF faced challenges from declining coal shipments and labor negotiations. Nonetheless, the railroad remains one of the most valuable transportation assets in North America.

Inflation

Inflation dominated economic headlines in 2022. The Federal Reserve raised interest rates aggressively to combat price increases that proved more persistent than initially expected.

For Berkshire, inflation is a mixed blessing. Our capital-intensive businesses face higher replacement costs. But our pricing power generally allows us to pass through cost increases. And higher interest rates increase returns on our cash holdings.

Looking Forward

Interest rates are now at levels that make sense. The era of free money appears to be ending. For disciplined capital allocators, this is a positive development.

We remain optimistic about America's future. The country's dynamism and entrepreneurial spirit are unmatched. Berkshire is positioned to participate in that future.


Warren E. Buffett
Chairman of the Board

February 25, 2023
